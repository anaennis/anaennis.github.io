About Me

I'm Ana Inés Ennis, an astrophysicist and data scientist with a Ph.D. in Astrophysics and 7+ years of experience working with large-scale datasets, machine learning, and scientific computing.

I specialize in building end-to-end data pipelines, developing automated analysis workflows, and applying ML techniques to complex, high-dimensional data. My work spans astronomical data engineering, spectral cube analysis, and GenAI-powered tools for research discovery.

I enjoy transforming messy, multi-instrument datasets into clean, actionable insights—and communicating results clearly to both technical and non-technical audiences.

Research

My research focuses on planetary nebulae, globular cluster systems, and galaxy evolution, with an emphasis on building automated pipelines for multi-instrument datasets and developing statistical tools for stellar population analysis. I also contribute to large survey collaborations and open-source astronomical software.

Planetary Nebulae as Stellar Population Tracers

Led a pilot study using MUSE integral-field spectroscopy to analyze planetary nebulae as probes of stellar population properties. Developed automated routines for emission-line detection, continuum subtraction, and Gaussian fitting. The spectral-line analysis code will be released publicly in summer 2026 to support reproducible research in the community.

Globular Cluster Systems & Automated Detection

Built automated pipelines for detecting and characterizing globular cluster systems across Gemini/GMOS, J-PLUS, and MUSE imaging. Applied PCA, Monte Carlo simulations, and photometric modeling to study galaxy assembly histories. The automated globular cluster detection code will also be made publicly available in summer 2026.

IRAF-Style Simulation Tools

Developed iraf_simil, a Python-based tool for generating IRAF-style simulated astronomical images to support testing, calibration, and pipeline validation. The project is open-source and available on GitHub: github.com/anaennis/iraf_simil.
